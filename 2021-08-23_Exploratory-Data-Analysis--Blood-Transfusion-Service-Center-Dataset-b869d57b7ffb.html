Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):

EDA is all about finding relationships and hidden patterns between the dataset. In Python, there are beautiful libraries for visualization like seaborn, matplotlib, plotly, etc, and Pandas, Numpy for Data Analysis. So, in this blog, you will see the use of these libraries for Exploratory Data Analysis.

The main reason for EDA is the analysis of our data before making any assumptions for further data processing. It helps us to find hidden patterns between the features, detection of outliers in the data, balancing of our dataset, finding missing value in features and types of data presents in attributes, etc.

Let's proceed with the step by step EDA analysis:

1. Dataset Information :

Link: https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Blood+Transfusion+Service+Center

Title: Blood Transfusion Service Center Data Set

Description: The dataset contains the information of blood donors, which is collected by the “Blood Transfusion Service Center” in Hsin-Chu City in Taiwan. The center passes their blood transfusion service bus to one university in Hsin-Chu City to gather blood donated about every three months. For more information related to the dataset follow( https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Blood+Transfusion+Service+Center) link.

Attribute Information:

Recency — months since the last donation

Frequency — total number of donation

Monetary — total blood donated in c.c.

Time — months since the first donation

whether he/she donated blood in March 2007 — a binary variable representing whether he/she donated blood in March 2007.

  • 1 stand for donating blood.
  • 0 stands for not donating blood.

12. Univariate, Bivariate and Multivariate Analysis

Let’s now explore our dataset with visualizations. Python has a visualization library, Seaborn, matplotlib and plotly, etc. It provides very attractive statistical graphs in order to perform both Univariate and Multivariate analysis.

12. 1. Univariate Analysis

The univariate analysis is used to describe, summarize, and analyze the dependency of a single feature/attribute, and how it will affect a dependent variable.

Univariate analysis helps us to analysis on Central Tendency (mean, mode and median), Spread of data (range, variance), Percentile, Quartiles (interquartile range), Standard deviation, PDF(Probability Density Function), CDF(Cumulative Distribution Function), etc.

Mean, Variance and Std-deviation

PDF(Probability Density Function)

The smooth approximation of the histogram is PDF. It is obtained from the KDE(Kernel Density Estimation) of the histogram. For more info(“https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kernel_density_estimation). The area under the PDF = 1. PDF at the point can think of the percentage of the total data points at this value x. or Probability of finding total data points at value x. In the histogram, the height of the bar explains the percentage of data points under the corresponding value x.

sns.FacetGrid(blood_transfusion,hue='Donated Blood',height = 5)\
.map(sns.distplot,'Recency (months)')\
. add_legend();
plt.show()

Observation:

Here, with 1 or 5 th Recency(months) are more likely to Donate Blood. There are very few chances of donated blood if there are 25 or more Recency(months).

sns.FacetGrid(blood_transfusion,hue='Donated Blood',height = 5)\

12. 2. Bivariate Analysis

The bivariate Analysis means there are two features. The analysis is related to find the relationship between the two features.

Scatter Plots

A scatter plot is a two-dimensional data visualization that uses dots to represent the values obtained for two different variables — one plotted along the x-axis and the other plotted along the y-axis.

blood_transfusion.plot(y='Recency (months)', x='Frequency (times)',kind='scatter');
plt.show()

Observation:

For Most of the Donated Blood, The Recency(months) is below ‘25’. But not able to separate between classes due to the same color. Let's take different colors.

sns.set_style("whitegrid")
sns.FacetGrid(blood_transfusion,hue='Donated Blood',size=5) \

Observation

Separating Frequency from Recency is much harder as they have considerable overlapping with each other.

A contour line is a function of two variables is a curve along which the function has a constant value. It is a cross-section of the three-dimensional graph.

13. Conclusions:

Donated Blood Time(months) and Recency (months) alone are not deciding factors for donated blood in blood transfusion. Yet, people less than 5 Recency(months) have more chance of donated Blood.

Blood Donated chance is inversely proportional to frequency(total number of donation), and Monetary(total blood donated in c.c.).

The objective of classifying whether he/she donated blood in March 2007 or not of new people(he/she) based on the given attribute is a difficult task as the data is imbalanced.
